The Forbidden Forest

i didnt plan to run. Not at first. It started with a step, just one, backward. 




Tigger noticed immediately.

His eyes always did.

“You’re thinking too much again,” said Tigger, his voice soft, almost soothing. “That’s usually when things get… difficult.”

“I need space,” I said.

“You have space,” he replied quickly. “All of this is yours.”

“That’s the problem.”

The words slipped out before I could stop them.

For a moment, neither of us moved.

The garden held its breath.

“You don’t mean that,” he said, quieter now.

“I do.”

I took another step back.

Then another.

The path behind me—one I didn’t remember seeing before—seemed to open wider, as if inviting me.

Or warning me.

“I think I want to leave,” I said.

The effect was immediate.

Tigger froze.

Not playfully.

Not theatrically.

Completely.

The kind of stillness that doesn’t belong to something alive.

“…You always say that part differently,” he murmured.

My chest tightened.

“What happens after I say it?” I asked.

He didn’t answer.

Instead—

He took a step forward.

And I felt it.

Not just movement—

Pressure.

Like the garden itself leaned in with him.

I turned—

And ran.
